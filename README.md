## 1) Blue Team (Defensive Security)

**What they do:** Protect systems, detect attacks, investigate incidents, and improve resilience.

**Common responsibilities:**
- Monitor alerts and logs (SOC workflows)
- Detect malicious behavior and threats
- Perform incident response and post-incident reviews
- Harden systems and reduce attack surface

**Suggested learning path:**
1. Networking, Linux/Windows basics, and SIEM fundamentals
2. Log analysis, threat detection, and incident handling
3. Threat hunting, detection engineering, and automation

### Useful links (Blue Team)
- MITRE ATT&CK: https://attack.mitre.org/
- LetsDefend: https://letsdefend.io/
- CyberDefenders Labs: https://cyberdefenders.org/
- Security Onion Docs: https://docs.securityonion.net/
- SANS DFIR Resources: https://www.sans.org/white-papers/?focus-area=digital-forensics-incident-response

## 2) Red Team (Offensive Security)

**What they do:** Simulate real attackers to identify weaknesses before adversaries exploit them.

**Common responsibilities:**
- Perform penetration tests and adversary simulations
- Assess web, cloud, network, and endpoint security
- Develop attack paths and proof-of-concept exploits
- Report findings with business impact and remediation guidance

**Suggested learning path:**
1. Networking, scripting, Linux, and web fundamentals
2. Vulnerability assessment and common exploit techniques
3. Advanced pentesting, lateral movement, and opsec

## 3) GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance)

**What they do:** Align security with business goals through policy, risk management, and compliance programs.

**Common responsibilities:**
- Build and maintain security policies and standards
- Manage risk registers and control assessments
- Support audits and compliance evidence collection
- Coordinate with legal, privacy, and business teams

**Suggested learning path:**
1. Security frameworks and control catalogs
2. Risk assessment methodologies and reporting
3. Audit preparation, governance metrics, and stakeholder communication

### Useful links (GRC)
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF): https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework
- NIST SP 800 Series: https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/sp
- ISO/IEC 27001 overview: https://www.iso.org/isoiec-27001-information-security.html
- CIS Controls: https://www.cisecurity.org/controls

## 4) Security Engineering & AppSec (Added area)

This is a key area often between Red/Blue/GRC: designing and building secure systems from the start.

**What they do:** Integrate security into architecture, code, CI/CD, cloud, and platform operations.

**Common responsibilities:**
- Secure design reviews and threat modeling
- Application security testing (SAST/DAST/SCA)
- Cloud and infrastructure hardening-as-code
- Security automation in development pipelines

**Suggested learning path:**
1. Secure coding and common vulnerability classes
2. Threat modeling and secure architecture patterns
3. DevSecOps tooling and cloud security engineering

### Useful links (Security Engineering & AppSec)
- OWASP Foundation Projects: https://owasp.org/projects/
